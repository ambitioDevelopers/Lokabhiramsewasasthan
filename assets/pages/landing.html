Our Mission

The sansthan is working for the promotion of Shri Ram Katha, Bhagwat and other religious and festival works. The sansthan is also promoting and carrying out activities such as marriage of poor girls, establishment of old age home, cow shelter and providing free education to the poor, disabled and disadvantaged children's of the society. Institution is also awakening the true sprit of nationalism and patriotisms among the people of the nation.

Read About Us:

The Lokabhiram Sewa sansthan is registered with the Registration Number: 3037, 2017- 18, under the Societies Registration Act 1860. Public service of the people's service, Shri Ram Katha, Bhagwat Katha, religious marriage as well as the marriage of poor girls, old age of ashram, promoting the importance of cows, making the elderly cows sheds in villages for the service of sick ,helpless, disabled cows; To educate the poor children and to awaken the spirit of nation welfare

The main goals of Lokabhiram Seva Sansthan:

  1. To develop the social, mental, physical, ethical, moral and intellectual characteristics of the people.
  2. To invite various scholars, saints, and Katha Vachak from time to time, to deliver lectures or discourse on Mahakavays, Epics and Vedas in order to provide benefit to the common people to live a satisfied life.
  3. Organizing cultural programs, spiritual programs from time to time.
  4. Arranging the knowledge building library and reading room.
  5. Conducting and as well as helping the families of the poor girls for there marriage and promoting the ideas of dowry free marriage.
  6. Arrangement of accommodation for visitors from outside and arranging free meals.
  7. Imparting free education to childrens belonging from Adivasi community, disable children, destitute background and other disadvantage sections of the society and also providing free meals and day boarding facilities.
  8. Conducting from time to time cultural programs, seminars, conferences, yoga programs, awareness camps, health camp and social awareness exhibitions and etc.
  9. To organize nation unity communal harmony camps and awaken the spirit of mutual harmony and world brotherhood among the people.
  10. Arranging free education for the poor girls.
  11. Establishment and arrangement of Janhitarth Vriddha Ashram, Widow Ashram, Short Stay Home
  12. To open the Gaushala in village and to promote cow protection, to provide care and medical care of sick, helpless, unclaimed and handicapped cows.
  13. To propagate the promotion of goods produced from cow and to promote the importance of cow's service in the public.
  14. To participate in those programs that is initiated by Gau Sewa Commission.
  15. To provide right direction and meaningful life to the directional less and stray people of the society, so that they can serve the country in the best possible manner.

Our Inspiration

Jagadguru Ram Bhadracharya

Jagadguru Ramanandacharya (born on January 14, 1950) is a Hindu religious leader, teacher, Sanskrit scholar, multilingual, poet, writer, reader commentator, philosopher, composer, singer, playwright and Katha artiste in Chitrakoot of India. He is one of the four existing Jagadguru Ramanandacharya and has organized this title since 1988.
