Our Mission

The establishment of an old Age Homes, protection of the disabled cows, promoting the importance of cows, the marriage of the poor girls as well as the religious services of the Lord Ram Katha, Bhagwat Katha, the religious work.To educate the poor children and to awaken the spirit of nation welfare.

The main goals of Lokabhiram Seva Sansthan:

  1. Develop social, mental, ethical, characteristic and intellectual development of the people.
  2. Invite the story readers from time to time and to benefit the common people through their discourses.
  3. Organizing cultural programs, spiritual programs from time to time.
  4. Arranging the knowledge building library and reading room.
  5. Helping the marriage of poor girls and motivating them for mass marriage and dowry marriage.
  6. Arrangement of accommodation for visitors from outside and arranging free meals.
  7. Children of the society, Adivasi, disabled, destitute, free education for children, arrangement of food, medical and housing.
  8. From time to time cultural programs, academic programs, seminars, conferences, seminars, conference seminars, yoga programs, awareness camps, pulse poles immunization, health camps, eye camps, blood donation camps, social awareness exhibitions Organizing etc.
  9. To organize nation unity communal harmony camps and awaken the spirit of mutual harmony and world brotherhood among the people.
  10. Arranging free education for the poor girls.
  11. Arrangement of Janhitarth Vriddha Ashram, Widow Ashram, Short Stay Home
  12. To open the Gaushala in village villages and to promote cow protection, to provide care and medical care of sick, helpless, unclaimed and handicapped cows.
  13. To propagate the promotion of goods produced from cow and to promote the importance of cow's service in the public,
  14. Participation in the programs by the Gauva Seva Commission through programs run by the Gau Seva Commission.
  15. Serving Mother India by giving stray and directionless society towards spirituality and providing right direction.

Our Inspiration

Jagadguru Ram Bhadracharya

Jagadguru Ramanandacharya (born on January 14, 1950) is a Hindu religious leader, teacher, Sanskrit scholar, multilingual, poet, writer, reader commentator, philosopher, composer, singer, playwright and Katha artiste in Chitrakoot of India. He is one of the four existing Jagadguru Ramanandacharya and has organized this title since 1988.
